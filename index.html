Bank Campaign Analysis

Introduction

The data chosen for this visualization analysis came from a direct marketing effort run by a Australian bank. The main way people were contacted was through phone calls. The goal of the effort was to get people to sign up for bank term deposits. The main goal is to use visualizations to find out what kind of people signed up for the term deposit and which months of the campaign were the most popular.

Results of the Campaign

To gauge the campaign's success, this visualization provides insights into the percentage and total number of individuals who subscribed or declined the term deposit offer. About 10% of the targeted audience subscribed, which, although appears low, reflects the reality of campaign efforts where numerous interactions yield modest conversions. A pie chart was chosen for this visualization as it effectively illustrates the proportion of subscribed versus not subscribed individuals.

Region Map for People Not-Subscribed

This visualization showcases the geographic distribution of non-subscriptions across different states. The analysis reveals higher non-subscription counts in New South Wales, Victoria, and Queensland, suggesting a potential need for revised marketing strategies in these regions.

Conversely, lower non-subscription counts are observed in Northern Territory, Tasmania, and South Australia, indicating lesser disengagement.

The geographic insight provided by this visualization is crucial for strategizing future marketing initiatives to improve engagement and subscriptions.

Subscribed People by Month and Personal Loan

This interactive line chart juxtaposes the monthly count of subscribed individuals against their personal loan status. A significant insight is that a larger portion of subscribers did not have personal loans, especially in May, a month where the campaign saw relative success.

This interactive chart provides an exact count of subscribers, delineating those with and without personal loans each month.
